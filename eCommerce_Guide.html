eCommerce Guide

The ItsOn eCommerce section of Service Design Center is where you configure and manage all aspects of your offerings, the products and services you sell to your customers. Service Design Center uses a number of concepts to help you with this configuration and management including:

  • Products, items that you offer for sale.
  • Fees, items that define charges to customers that are (usually) related to the products they buy.
  • Product Bundles, items that combine multiple products and/or fees.
  • Product Options, 
  • Price Lists, the currency units that you accept.
  • Sandboxes, how you manage the process of creating and testing the items you want in your store. 

Nomenclature and Organization

The eCommerce section interacts with the policies section of Service Design Center, so it's important to understand what policies do.

Policies In Service Design Center

Service Design Center is where you design and manage your policies. It provides 3 types of policies: service, data session, and WiFi. All 3 types are used for carrier network management. Service policies are also used as the basis for plans that are configured and deployed in the eCommerce section and sold in your store. 

Policies are added to and configured in 2 types of entities in Service Design Center. One type, services (publishable features), is used for cellular network management. These services can be deployed to all ItsOn-powered devices on the network. The other type, plans (purchasable features), is what is made available to the eCommerce section. 

About eCommerce In Service Design Center

At its heart, the ItsOn eCommerce section of Service Design Center is a store, a virtual store. But it is also so much more. It helps you manage products and their arrangement, point-of-sale, signage, marketing collateral, and more.

It is fundamentally a content management and change management program. The content is everything about what you offer your customers and what you use to describe and sell the offerings. The change management organizes all your changes in sandboxes and allows you to move them through a draft-promote-approve-deploy workflow.
