Plan Behavior Settings Examples

The flexibility of a product's settings means that you can create hundreds of combinations of plan behavior. This topic gives some examples of desired product behavior and how you would configure Behavior and other settings to ensure that behavior. 

Some of these scenarios require additional product settings to be configured in specific ways to have the desired behavior. This topic focuses on how specific settings in the Receipt/Sharing/Assigning and the Behavior sections of the General tab work together.

Basic Monthly Data Plan

You want to offer basic monthly data plans for single devices, perhaps with different caps for different prices, but their behavior would be the same-and (relatively) simple. Here are the settings and the reason for each:

Setting Value Reason
Customer can buy for Customer's account only This plan is for the subscriber's account only.
Allocation Exclusive Only the purchaser can use the plan.
Share Method n/a The Share Method setting is not used when the Allocation setting is Exclusive.
Share Type n/a The Share Type setting is not used when the Allocation setting is Exclusive.
Assignment Method Assignable at purchase only  
Number of Cycles Unlimited

The purchaser of the plan receives and pays for it again at the end of each period. The plan renews until it is canceled by the purchaser, even if you discontinue the plan.
Aligned to account cycle Yes Making monthly charges aligned with a customer's account cycle makes your accounting and your customer's understanding easiest because your customers pay just once per cycle.
Cycle Duration n/a When a plan is aligned to the account cycle, the duration equals the account cycle length, which can be anywhere from 28 to 31 days for monthly plans.
Prorated on first cycle Yes

Both the amount paid and the amount of usage available is prorated. For example, an account cycle that starts on the 1st, and a 30MB data plan for $30 bought on the 20th. For the last 10 days of the account cycle, the customer pays $10 for 10MB of data usage.

10-Day Facebook Plan

Most plans that you offer limit what your users can receive along two axes: amount and time. Some of the Behavior settings address specifics for the time axis. 

Setting Value Reason
Customer can buy for Customer's account only The idea is that because the plan duration is short, either a subscriber on the account buys it for just themselves, or the account holder buys it for just one subscriber on the account. A classic example would be a parent buying the plan for a child on a family account. So the plan can't be passed around from subscriber to subscriber (Reassignable for the Assignment Method setting), nor can the plan be consumed by multiple subscribers (Shareable for the Allocation setting).
Allocation Assignable For accounts with just one subscriber, only the purchaser gets the plan allocation. For accounts wth more that one subscriber, the purchaser can designate a different subscriber in the account to get the plan allocation, at which point that subscriber is the only subscriber that can consume the plan allocation.
Share Method n/a The Share Method setting is not used when the Allocation setting is Exclusive.
Share Type n/a The Share Type setting is not used when the Allocation setting is Exclusive.
Assignment Method Assignable at purchase only The ability to consume allocation from the plan cannot be passed from subscriber to subscriber in the account.
Number of Cycles Limited to 1 This plan is not automatically renewed at the end of the 10 days.
Aligned to account cycle n/a This plan is valid for 10 days from its purchase date.
Cycle duration 10 days This one's easy. The cycle length.
Prorated on first cycle n/a Proration applies only to renewable plans.

90 Days of Service: Recurring vs. Non-recurring

You can offer a set amount of service in different ways. This example shows the differences in 90 days of service offered in a 30-day plan that renews 2 times (finite recurring) vs. a 90-day plan. In this example, you want to offer up to 30GB of data during the 90 days.

Setting Value Reason
Customer can buy for any For this scenario, the choice here doesn't matter.
Allocation any For this scenario, the choice here doesn't matter.
Share Method any For this scenario, the choice here doesn't matter.
Share Type any For this scenario, the choice here doesn't matter.
Assignment Method n/a For this scenario, the choice here doesn't matter.
Number of Cycles Limited to 3 For the 30-day plan. The idea is to give the same amount of time as the 90-day plan.
Limited to 1 For the 90-day plan.
Aligned to account cycle No

The cycle for the plan and payment start on the purchase date.

If the plan is not free, customers that do not purchase the plan on the first day of their account cycle will be charged on the plan cycle date, which can result in charges for different plans at different times each month.

But if you align the plan to the account cycle and prorate the first month, customers will not get the full 90 days (unless they purchase the plan on the first day of their account cycle) because the plan can be renewed only twice.

Your business and customer needs should dictate when you want plans configured like this. Consider limiting this configuration to plans that you do not charge for.
Cycle duration 30 days For the 30-day plan. The plan lasts 30 days, and the renewable setting allows to to occur 3 times (the initial, plus 2 renewals), for 90 days total.
90 days For the 90-day plan.
Prorated on first cycle n/a If the plan is not aligned to the account cycle, there is no proration.
Data usage Limited to 10,240 megabytes For the 30-day plan.
Limited to 30,720 megabytes For the 90-day plan.

The different settings determine how much data a user can consume, and when. In the 30-day plan scenario, users get 10GB per 30 days. If they use 10GB in the first day, they have no more data under the plan until the 30 days is complete. Conversely, if they use only 10MB of data during the entirety of the 30 days, they lose all the rest of the 10GB allocated for that 30 days and the "counter" begins again at the start of the next 30-day cycle.

In the 90-day plan scenario, users can consume the 30GB of data at any time during the 90 days of the plan.
